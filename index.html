AVA House

A community-led, culturally responsive support service dedicated to strengthening foster and kinship care placements while empowering young people to transition into independent, successful futures.

We work alongside carers, families, and communities to provide practical, emotional, and cultural support — ensuring no young person is left without connection, stability, or opportunity.

Make a Referral Our Story

Our Mission

To build stronger placements, stronger carers, and stronger futures for young people in foster and kinship care.

Our Vision

A future where every young person leaves care equipped, confident, and connected to culture, community, and opportunity.

Our Services

Carer Recruitment & Engagement

  • • Community outreach (churches, cultural groups)
  • • Information sessions
  • • Carer pathway guidance

Carer Support Hub

  • • Monthly support groups
  • • One-on-one case support
  • • Crisis and after-hours assistance
  • • Advocacy and system navigation

Placement Stabilisation

  • • Early intervention support
  • • Mediation between carers and young people
  • • Behaviour and wellbeing support

Youth Transition Program (15–18 years)

  • • Employment readiness
  • • Driver's licence program
  • • Life skills (budgeting, cooking, renting)
  • • Certifications (First Aid, White Card)

Cultural Mentorship

  • • Connection to community leaders and mentors
  • • Identity and belonging support
  • • Faith-based and cultural guidance
CRITICAL

Emergency & After-Hours Support

Responsive, short-term emergency placement support when immediate placements are unavailable.

  • • Safe, supervised emergency accommodation
  • • Support during crisis placements
  • • Stabilise and transition to longer-term carers

Our Model of Care

The AVA House 4 Pillar Model

1

Recruit

We identify, engage, and support culturally appropriate foster and kinship carers through strong community connections.

2

Support

We provide wraparound support to carers to reduce burnout and improve placement stability.

3

Stabilise

We intervene early to prevent placement breakdowns and support both carers and young people.

4

Future

We equip young people with the skills, qualifications, and pathways needed for independence.
